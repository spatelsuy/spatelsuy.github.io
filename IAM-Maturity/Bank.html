IAM Maturity Assessment – Version 1 (Banking Scope)

This IAM Maturity Assessment provides a structured, AI-assisted way to evaluate how effectively an organization manages identities and access. Version 1 (V1) is intentionally scoped to the Banking industry, where regulatory expectations and risk tolerance are high.

What This Assessment Covers

  • Identity Lifecycle Management – Joiner, Mover, Leaver processes
  • Access Governance – Access requests, approvals, and reviews
  • Authentication & MFA – Authentication strength and MFA usage
  • Privileged Access Management – Control of privileged accounts
  • Monitoring & Logging – Visibility and oversight of IAM activity

How Maturity Is Measured

Users select the option that best reflects their current practice for each question. Each option represents a baseline maturity level (1–5). An AI-based assessment agent then evaluates the selection using banking-aligned expectations, regulatory context, and IAM architecture to determine a final maturity score.

Maturity Levels

LevelDescription
1Ad-hoc, manual, inconsistent
2Partially implemented, limited consistency
3Defined, documented, repeatable
4Managed, integrated, measured
5Optimized, automated, continuously improved

Framework Alignment

This assessment is aligned with NIST CSF (PR.AA), ISO/IEC 27001 (Annex A.9), and the CRI Identity Risk framework. It provides maturity insight but does not replace formal audits or certifications.

Version Scope

V1 is limited to the Banking industry. Future versions will expand coverage to additional industries and include enhanced evidence-based scoring.
