IAM Maturity Assessment

What is IAM?

Identity and Access Management (IAM) is the framework of policies, processes, and technologies used to manage digital identities and control access to systems, applications, and data. It ensures that the right people have the right access at the right time.

What is IAM Maturity & Why it Matters?

IAM Maturity measures how effectively an organization manages identities and access. Higher maturity means consistent, predictable, and risk-aware IAM processes. Assessing maturity helps organizations:

  • Identify gaps and risks in identity management
  • Plan improvements and automation
  • Align IAM with industry frameworks (NIST, CRI, ISO)
  • Support audits and compliance efforts

How IAM Maturity is Calculated

Maturity is calculated by:

  1. Defining IAM domains (areas of IAM capability)
  2. Assessing each domain through specific questions
  3. Scoring each question from 1 to 5
  4. Calculating the average score per domain
  5. Calculating overall IAM maturity by averaging domain scores
  6. Mapping the score to a maturity level (Level 1–5)

Domains Considered in IAM Maturity

  • Identity Lifecycle Management (Joiner-Mover-Leaver)
  • Access Governance (IGA, approvals, access reviews)
  • Authentication & MFA
  • Privileged Access Management (PAM)
  • Monitoring & Logging

Maturity Score Measurement

Each question is scored 1–5. Definitions:

ScoreMeaning
1Ad-hoc, manual, inconsistent
2Partially implemented, limited consistency
3Defined, documented, repeatable
4Managed, measured, enforced
5Optimized, automated, continuously improved
